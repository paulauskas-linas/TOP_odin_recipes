Varškės spurgos - Lithuanian Fried Cottage Cheese Doughnuts

All over the world you could find some sort of fried doughnuts. Every country, no, every ethnical group added their twist to doughnut recipe. Lithuania also did the same and this is the idea of adding cottage cheese. Varškės spurgos (lit. cottage cheese doughnuts) are made like any other fried dougnut. You can find all around the country in the restaurants, even pre-made in some grocery shops. In restaurants and at home it is served warm covered in sugar powder.

Instructions

Main dish

  1. In bigger bowl mix cottage cheese with eggs and sugar.
  2. Add vanilla extract.
  3. In separate bowl mix sour cream with baking soda
  4. Transfer sour cream to main bowl. Mix well.
  5. Add a couple of tablespoons of strong transparent liquer. It helps with preventing the oil from seeping in to the doughnut.
  6. Add the flour. Mix it well. The dough should be neither firm nor watery and sticky to the hand.
  7. In deep pot preheat the oil to 160-170 C (320-340 F).
  8. Oil your hands, form round balls from dough and carefully put the in to the hot oil.
  9. Once the doughnuts float, become brown color and when hit with the spoon produces a dull sound - they are ready.
  10. Lay fried doughnuts on the towel paper to dry off the excess fat.
  11. Repeat the process for the rest of the dough.
  12. If needed, change the oil.
  13. Once done, cover the doughnuts with powdered sugar.
  14. Clean up everything

Ingredients

Varškės spurgos

  • 400 gr (1,5 cups) of cottage cheese
  • 2 medium sized eggs
  • 4 tablespoons of sugar (or however you want)
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon of sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • couple of tablespoons of liquer (e.g. vodka)
  • 8 tablespoons of flour
  • a bottle of vegetable oil
  • powdered sugar

Try not to eat all of the them while cooking. Tastes best when shared with loved ones. Enjoy!
